# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers, pledge to make participation in our community
harassment-free for everyone regardless of age, visible or invisible disability,
ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, level of experience,
education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion,
sexual orientation, or other dimension of identity.

We pledge to interact in ways that foster an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive,
and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Positive behavior examples include empathy and kindness toward others, respectful
disagreement, accepting responsibility when appropriate, focusing on constructive
benefit to the community, and supporting an environment aligned with stated project
scientific ethics (pre-registered evidence boundaries, reproducibility honesty).

Examples of unacceptable behavior include sexualized comments or harassment of any kind,
personal or political insults or attacks, disruptive trolling or spam, publishing others’
personal information without permission, advocating harm, and other inappropriate conduct.

Project maintainers will define and clarify standards of acceptable behavior within the
narrow scope of benchmarking and evidence reporting as needed via maintainers-only
responses to issues.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers clarify project norms and enforce this Code fairly and consistently using
responses they judge appropriate — including warnings, removals of contributions, or bans
when serious or repeated misconduct occurs.

Maintainers prioritize safety and inclusivity alongside project integrity boundaries
defined in README and SECURITY policy.

## Scope

This applies to all interaction spaces tied to contributing to Open Wave Public
Benchmarks — issues, discussions, PRs, and any project communications where contributors
meet.

## Enforcement

Report suspected violations privately by opening **GitHub Issues** with the title prefix

`[conduct]`

Maintainership will acknowledge valid reports promptly when possible and investigate
according to severity while protecting reporter privacy reasonably.

Emergency abuse or imminent harm may also use GitHub abuse reporting tooling.

Repeated bad-faith filings about others also violate this Code.
